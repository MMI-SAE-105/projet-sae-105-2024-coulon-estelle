A Timeless Vision

History

From Protection to Fashion

From the first Inuit snow goggles, designed to protect the eyes from the sun's rays reflected by the snow, to contemporary models combining technology and design, sunglasses have evolved to meet various needs and reflect the cultural trends of each era.

year 100

Inuit mask that blocks the sun's rays reflected by the snow.

Roman Era

Emerald used against the sun by Emperor Nero to watch gladiator fights.

12th century

Smoke quartz glasses used by the Japanese.

1752

First pair of glasses with tinted glass by the English.

1920

First polarized flap scope.

2024

Current Prada brand glasses.

FUTURE

The glasses of the future will be equipped with artificial intelligence (AI). They will transform our daily lives. They will be able to translate languages in real time, provide information about the places visited, and monitor sports performance. In terms of safety, they will detect obstacles and warn of potential dangers. For entertainment, they will offer immersive augmented reality experiences. Customizable, they will adapt to each user's preferences, making our lives more connected and rewarding.

In addition, these glasses will feature advanced UV protections, providing increased defense against harmful ultraviolet rays. With improved filtration technologies, they will effectively protect the eyes from damage caused by prolonged exposure to the sun.

By combining style, technology and health, the glasses of the future will be an essential accessory for a modern and secure life.
